
MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

This Master Service Agreement (“Agreement”) is entered into as of October 17, 2025, by and between:

Alpha Consulting Pvt. Ltd., a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, having its registered office at 24 Business Park, Bengaluru, India (hereinafter referred to as the “Service Provider”),
and
Beta Innovations Ltd., a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, having its registered office at 19 Tech Avenue, Hyderabad, India (hereinafter referred to as the “Client”).

The Service Provider and the Client may hereinafter be collectively referred to as the “Parties” and individually as a “Party.”

1. SCOPE OF SERVICES
   1.1 The Service Provider shall provide management consulting, technology integration, and strategic advisory services to the Client as described in Annexure A (“Services”).
   1.2 The Services shall be performed with due professional skill, care, and diligence and in accordance with applicable industry standards.
   1.3 The Service Provider shall ensure that all personnel assigned to perform the Services possess the required expertise and qualifications.



2. TERM AND COMMENCEMENT
   2.1 This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall remain in force for a period of one (1) year, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Agreement.
   2.2 The Parties may renew this Agreement for successive terms upon mutual written consent.



3. PAYMENT TERMS
   3.1 The Client shall pay the Service Provider a professional fee of INR 5,00,000 per project milestone, as outlined in Annexure B.
   3.2 All invoices shall be payable within thirty (30) days from the date of receipt.
   3.3 Any delay in payment shall attract interest at the rate of 2% per month, compounded monthly, until payment is made in full.
   3.4 Taxes, if applicable, shall be borne by the Client.



4. CONFIDENTIALITY
   4.1 Each Party agrees to maintain the confidentiality of all confidential or proprietary information disclosed by the other Party, whether in written, oral, or electronic form.
   4.2 Confidential Information shall not include information that:
   (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement;
   (b) is received lawfully from a third party without restriction; or
   (c) was independently developed without reference to the Confidential Information.
   4.3 Each Party shall use the other Party’s Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing its obligations under this Agreement.
   4.4 This clause shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for two (2) years.



5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS
   5.1 All deliverables, reports, data, and materials created under this Agreement (“Work Product”) shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Client upon full payment.
   5.2 The Service Provider shall retain ownership of its pre-existing materials, tools, and methodologies, but grants the Client a perpetual, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use such materials solely for internal purposes.
   5.3 The Service Provider shall not use, copy, or distribute the Work Product or any portion thereof without prior written consent from the Client.


6. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES
   6.1 Each Party represents that it has full authority to enter into and perform this Agreement.
   6.2 The Service Provider warrants that the Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner.
   6.3 The Client acknowledges that the Service Provider does not guarantee specific business outcomes or results.
   6.4 Except as expressly stated, all warranties, express or implied, including warranties of merchantability or fitness for a particular purpose, are disclaimed.



7. INDEMNIFICATION
   7.1 The Service Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Client, its officers, directors, and employees against any claims, damages, or liabilities arising from (a) breach of this Agreement, (b) negligence or misconduct, or (c) infringement of third-party intellectual property rights.
   7.2 The Client shall indemnify the Service Provider for any claims arising from the Client’s use of the Services in violation of applicable law.



8. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
   8.1 Except for liability arising from willful misconduct, confidentiality breach, or intellectual property infringement, each Party’s total liability shall not exceed the total amount paid under this Agreement.
   8.2 Neither Party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages, including loss of profits or business interruption.



9. TERMINATION
   9.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing thirty (30) days’ written notice to the other Party.
   9.2 The Client may terminate immediately if the Service Provider fails to remedy a material breach within fifteen (15) days of receiving written notice.
   9.3 Upon termination, the Service Provider shall return or destroy all Confidential Information and deliver all completed Work Product to the Client.
   9.4 Termination shall not relieve the Client of its obligation to pay for Services performed up to the date of termination.


10. FORCE MAJEURE
    Neither Party shall be liable for failure to perform obligations due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including acts of God, natural disasters, war, strikes, pandemics, or governmental restrictions. Performance shall resume as soon as practicable after the cessation of such event.



11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
    11.1 The Parties shall attempt to resolve all disputes through good faith negotiation.
    11.2 If no resolution is reached within thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.
    11.3 The arbitration shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator appointed by mutual agreement. The seat of arbitration shall be Bengaluru, India, and proceedings shall be conducted in English.
    11.4 The award shall be final and binding on both Parties.



12. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION
    This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India. The courts of Bengaluru shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any disputes not resolved by arbitration.



13. NOTICES
    All notices or communications shall be in writing and delivered by hand, courier, or registered email to the respective addresses mentioned herein. Notice shall be deemed received upon acknowledgment of delivery.



14. ASSIGNMENT
    Neither Party may assign or transfer its rights or obligations under this Agreement without prior written consent from the other Party, except to an affiliate or successor entity.



15. SEVERABILITY
    If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.



16. ENTIRE AGREEMENT
    This Agreement, along with its annexures, constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, or communications.



17. AMENDMENT
    This Agreement may only be amended or modified by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.



18. COUNTERPARTS
    This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.
